About Me

Hi, I'm Brooks Wimer, a recent Computer Science graduate from Boston University with a 3.74 GPA, driven by a passion for solving real-world problems through technology. My journey spans from developing software tools for New Englands power grid to building low-latency audio distribution systems.

I've created several innovative projects that showcase my technical abilities, including Foodpall - a functioning prototype of a food delivery application, SyncSonic - a multi-speaker Bluetooth audio orchestrator, and Kitbash - a customizable game controller app. Beyond academics, I've gained hands-on experience as a Software Development Intern at ISO New England, where I built tools for managing the region's power grid.

I also served as a Teacher's Assistant for C# full-stack app development class at Boston University, played on the Club Roller Hockey Team, was a memeber of the Deans Advisory Board, and was elected to be a peer tutor by my professors. I'm proficient in Python, R, Java, and the C-suite, with growing expertise in SQL, React, and Linux.

I believe in merging creativity with technology, whether it's building comprehensive tools, contributing to the energy sector, or collaborating with teams to create impactful software. I am looking for a role where I can learn a lot about technology and engage in creative problem solving.
